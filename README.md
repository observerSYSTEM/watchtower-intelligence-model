## Core Principles

### 1. Observation Before Detection
Detection is reactive by nature.

Watchtower prioritises continuous observation of behaviours, relationships, and evolution before threats reach an actionable stage.

---

### 2. Threats as Systems, Not Events
Watchtower models hostile activity as **Threat Constellations** â€” groups of related digital artifacts that together form a coordinated system.

Individually these signals may appear weak.  
Collectively they reveal intent.

---

### 3. Cloud-Native Threat Modelling
Modern threats are cloud-native.

They rely on:
- elastic infrastructure
- rapid redeployment
- automated certificates
- platform identities
- distributed hosting providers

Watchtower treats the cloud itself as a behavioural surface â€” not merely an execution environment.

---

### 4. Temporal Intelligence
In Watchtower, time is not metadata.

Burst activity, dormancy periods, reappearance intervals, and migration timing are treated as first-class intelligence signals.

---

### 5. Explainable Intelligence Outputs
Watchtower does not aim to produce alerts alone.

It produces intelligence:
- how a threat system formed
- which components are related
- how it migrated
- where it is likely to reappear

This enables analysts and architects to reason about risk â€” not simply react to noise.
